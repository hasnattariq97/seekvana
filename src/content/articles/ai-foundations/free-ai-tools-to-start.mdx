You can do everything in the [Getting Started course](/paths/getting-started) for free. No credit card. No trial that expires. Just a free account.

That is the honest answer, and it is worth saying clearly before anything else. A lot of articles on this topic quietly nudge you toward paid plans. This one does not — I have seen too many beginners delay getting started because they thought they needed to spend money first.

If you have not already, it helps to know [what AI actually is](/library/ai-foundations/what-is-ai) before diving into the tools — but you can read this lesson in any order.

> **Key Takeaways**
> - You need exactly one tool to complete Module 01: Claude at claude.ai (free account)
> - Claude, ChatGPT, and Gemini all have genuinely useful free tiers in 2026

## Do I need more than one AI tool to get started?

For Module 01 of the Getting Started course, you need **Claude at claude.ai**. That is it. Create a free account, and you have everything the lessons require.

You do not need to install anything. You do not need an API key. You do not need to give anyone a credit card number.

The free tier of Claude gives you access to Claude 3.5 Sonnet, a genuinely capable model, with a daily message limit that resets every 24 hours. For learning purposes, that is more than enough.

<Tip>
  If you already have a ChatGPT or Gemini account, those work fine too. The course examples use Claude, but the concepts apply to any of the major chatbots.
</Tip>

## Free AI tools for beginners: what each tier gives you

Here is the honest breakdown of what you get without paying, as of mid-2026:

<a href="https://claude.ai" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer"><strong>Claude</strong></a>
- Model: Claude 3.5 Sonnet
- Message limit: Limited messages per day, resets every 24 hours
- Context: 200k tokens (you can paste long documents)
- No credit card required
- Paid adds: more daily messages, Projects (persistent memory), access to Claude Opus

<a href="https://chat.openai.com" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer"><strong>ChatGPT</strong></a>
- Model: GPT-4o with daily limits, plus image generation
- Hits limits faster than Claude on the free tier
- Context: 128k tokens
- No credit card required
- Paid adds: unlimited GPT-4o, access to the o1 reasoning model

<a href="https://gemini.google.com" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer"><strong>Gemini</strong></a>
- Model: Gemini 1.5 Pro
- Context: 1 million tokens, the most generous free tier for long documents
- Requires a Google account (most people already have one)
- Paid adds: Gemini Ultra, deeper Google Workspace integration

<a href="https://x.ai/grok" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer"><strong>Grok</strong></a>
- Model: Grok-2
- Requires an X (Twitter) account
- Best for real-time news and X-platform content
- Paid adds: faster responses, more features

All four are worth knowing about. But for this course, Claude is the one we will use.

The reason to care about these differences is not to pick a winner — it is to know what to reach for when you hit a limit. Gemini's 1M context window, for instance, means you can paste an entire book into a conversation and ask questions about it, all for free.

For a deeper look at how the models compare beyond their free tiers, see our [full comparison of ChatGPT, Claude, and Gemini](/library/ai-tools/chatgpt-vs-claude-vs-gemini).

## Do I need the Claude API to start?

<ArticleImage
  src="/images/articles/ai-foundations/free-ai-tools-to-start/api-vs-chat.webp"
  alt="Two doorways side by side: an open door leading to a friendly chat interface, and a closed door with code symbols suggesting developer tools"
  caption="claude.ai (left) is the chat app — it's all you need. The Claude API (right) is for building your own apps. They are completely separate."
/>

You may have seen mentions of the **Claude API**, this is different from claude.ai. The API is for developers who want to build their own applications using Claude's capabilities.

For the Getting Started course, you do not need it. When you are ready to start building with AI (that comes later in the Launchpad curriculum), the API gives you $5 in free credits to start, then charges based on usage, no subscription required.

For now, forget the API exists. Claude at claude.ai is your tool.

<Note>
  The Claude API and claude.ai are completely separate products. You can use claude.ai forever without ever touching the API.
</Note>

## When should I actually pay for an AI tool?

Paid plans are not a scam. They genuinely add value, just not value you need right now.

The main things paid tiers add:

- **More messages per day**, useful if you are using AI for work, not just learning
- **Faster access during peak hours**, free tiers sometimes throttle when servers are busy
- **Advanced models**, Claude Opus, GPT-4o unlimited, Gemini Ultra
- **Memory and projects**, Claude's Projects feature lets the AI remember context across conversations

None of those things matter for learning the basics. When you find yourself genuinely blocked by a free tier limit, that is the signal to consider upgrading. It should feel like a natural next step, not a pressure.

<FAQ>
  <FAQItem question="Is the free version of Claude good enough for real work?">
    For learning and most everyday tasks, yes. Claude 3.5 Sonnet on the free tier handles writing, analysis, explanations, and code well. The main constraint is the daily message limit. If you are doing heavy professional use, dozens of long conversations per day, a paid plan becomes worth it. For a beginner working through a course, the free tier is more than sufficient.
  </FAQItem>
  <FAQItem question="What happens when I hit the free tier message limit?">
    The limit resets the next day. Claude will tell you when you have reached your limit for the day. You can either wait until tomorrow, switch to Gemini or ChatGPT for the rest of the day, or (if it keeps happening) consider upgrading. It is not an emergency, just a daily cap.
  </FAQItem>
  <FAQItem question="Do I need a different account for the Claude API versus claude.ai?">
    They use different platforms. claude.ai is the chat interface, you sign up at claude.ai. The Claude API requires a separate account at console.anthropic.com and is billed based on usage. You can have both, or neither, or just one. For this course, you only need the claude.ai chat account.
  </FAQItem>
  <FAQItem question="Is Gemini really free with a Google account?">
    Yes. If you have a Gmail account, you already have access to Gemini at gemini.google.com, no additional signup needed. The free tier runs Gemini 1.5 Pro with a 1 million token context window, which is the most generous free context window of any major chatbot. For document-heavy tasks, it is a strong free option.
  </FAQItem>
</FAQ>
